Cookie Policy

Cookie Policy

Last updated: May 2026

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that websites store on your device when you visit them. Think of them as digital sticky notes that help our dermatology website remember your preferences and provide you with a better browsing experience. These files contain no personal information that could identify you and cannot harm your computer.

Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to enhance your experience on our dermatology website. They help us remember your skin type preferences, track which articles about acne or skincare routines you’ve read, and ensure our medical content loads quickly for you. Cookies also help us understand which dermatology topics are most popular with our visitors, allowing us to create more helpful content about conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and anti-aging treatments.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies: These ensure our website functions properly, like keeping you logged in when accessing personalized skin assessment tools or maintaining your language preferences.

Analytics Cookies: We track how visitors use our dermatology content, which articles about skin conditions get the most views, and how long people spend reading about treatments like retinoids or sunscreen recommendations.

Affiliate Cookies: When you click links to recommended skincare products or dermatology books, these cookies help us earn a small commission if you make a purchase, supporting our free educational content.

Advertising Cookies: These show you relevant ads for dermatology services, skincare products, or medical equipment that might interest someone researching skin health.

Third Party Cookies

We work with trusted partners who may also set cookies on your device. Google Analytics helps us understand visitor behavior and popular dermatology topics. Affiliate networks like Amazon Associates track referrals when you purchase recommended skincare products. Advertising platforms may show you relevant ads for dermatology services or treatments you’ve shown interest in while browsing our site.

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have full control over cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline them if you prefer. Visit your browser’s help section for instructions. You can also opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing their browser add-on. For advertising cookies, visit the Digital Advertising Alliance’s opt-out page. Note that blocking certain cookies may affect how our dermatology content displays or functions.

Cookie Duration

Session Cookies: These temporary cookies disappear when you close your browser. They help maintain your session while browsing our dermatology articles.

Persistent Cookies: These remain on your device for a set period (typically 30 days to 2 years) and remember your preferences between visits, like your preferred skin type settings or bookmarked articles about specific skin conditions.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy occasionally to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Any updates will be posted on this page with a revised effective date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies on our dermatology website.

Contact Us

If you have questions about our use of cookies or this policy, please don’t hesitate to contact us. We’re here to help and ensure your privacy concerns are addressed promptly.
