Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Introduction

Welcome to dermatology (http://dermat.local), a content website dedicated to providing reliable information about skin health, dermatological conditions, and skincare guidance. We are committed to helping patients make informed decisions about their skin care while protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information when you visit our site.

Information We Collect

We collect several types of information to improve your experience on our dermatology website:

Automatically Collected Information: When you visit our site, we automatically collect technical information including your IP address, browser type, device information, pages viewed, time spent on pages, and referring websites through analytics tools.

Contact Form Data: If you contact us through our contact forms, we collect your name, email address, and any information you choose to share about your skin concerns or questions.

Newsletter Subscriptions: If you subscribe to our dermatology newsletter, we collect your email address and subscription preferences.

Cookies and Tracking: We use cookies to track your browsing behavior, remember your preferences, and measure the effectiveness of our content and any affiliate links you may click.

How We Use Your Information

We use your information for several purposes related to improving our dermatology content and services:

Content Improvement: We analyze which dermatology topics and skin care articles are most popular to create more relevant content for patients seeking skin health information.

Website Analytics: We track user behavior to understand how visitors navigate our site, which helps us improve the user experience and make our dermatology resources easier to find.

Communication: We use contact information to respond to your questions about skin conditions or our content, and to send newsletters about dermatology topics if you’ve subscribed.

Affiliate Program Tracking: We track clicks and purchases made through affiliate links to skincare products and dermatology-related services we recommend.

Affiliate Disclosure

This dermatology website participates in various affiliate marketing programs. This means we may earn commissions when you click on links to skincare products, dermatology treatments, or related services and make purchases. These affiliate relationships help support our ability to provide free, high-quality dermatology content to our visitors.

We only recommend products and services we believe may be beneficial for skin health. However, our affiliate relationships do not influence our editorial content or medical information. Always consult with a qualified dermatologist before starting any new skincare regimen or treatment.

Cookies

Our dermatology website uses several types of cookies:

Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled.

Analytics Cookies: We use cookies to track which dermatology articles you read and how you navigate our site, helping us understand what skin health information is most valuable to our visitors.

Affiliate Tracking Cookies: These cookies track when you click on affiliate links to skincare products or dermatology services, allowing us to earn commissions on qualifying purchases.

Preference Cookies: These remember your preferences for how you want to view our dermatology content.

You can control cookie settings through your browser, though disabling certain cookies may affect your experience on our site.

Third Party Services

We work with several third-party services to operate our dermatology website:

Google Analytics: We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our dermatology content and which skin care topics are most popular.

Affiliate Networks: We participate in affiliate programs with companies that sell skincare products and dermatology-related services.

Email Service Providers: We use third-party services to send newsletters about dermatology topics and skin health updates.

These third parties have their own privacy policies and may collect additional information about your visits to our site and others.

Your Rights

You have several rights regarding your personal information:

Access: You can request to know what personal information we have collected about you during your visits to our dermatology site.

Correction: You can ask us to correct any inaccurate personal information we may have.

Deletion: You can request that we delete your personal information, subject to certain legal requirements.

Opt-out: You can unsubscribe from our dermatology newsletters at any time using the unsubscribe link in any email.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the Contact section below.

Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy. Contact form submissions and email addresses are typically retained for up to three years unless you request deletion. Analytics data is retained according to our analytics provider’s standard retention periods, typically 26 months for Google Analytics.

Children’s Privacy

Our dermatology website is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under 13, we will delete that information as quickly as possible. If you believe we may have collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. When we make changes, we will update the effective date and post the revised Privacy Policy on this page. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.

Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, our data practices, or your rights regarding your personal information, please don’t hesitate to contact us. You can reach us through our contact page, where we’ll be happy to address any concerns you may have about privacy or any other aspect of our dermatology website.
