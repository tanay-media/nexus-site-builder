Oral Medications for Skin Conditions

Systemic prescription medications including antibiotics, antifungals, and immunosuppressants for severe skin conditions

When topical treatments aren't enough, oral medications can provide powerful, systemic relief for challenging skin conditions. These prescription medications work from within your body to target inflammation, infection, hormonal imbalances, and immune responses that contribute to various dermatological problems. From severe acne that resists topical therapy to autoimmune skin diseases, oral treatments offer solutions when other approaches fall short.

This section covers the major categories of oral medications used in dermatology, including antibiotics for acne and infections, immunosuppressants for severe inflammatory conditions, and specialized treatments like isotretinoin and biologics. You'll find detailed information about how these medications work, what conditions they treat, and what to expect during treatment.

  • Oral antibiotics for treating acne and bacterial skin infections
  • Isotretinoin for severe, treatment-resistant acne
  • Immunosuppressive medications for autoimmune and inflammatory skin diseases
  • Hormonal treatments for women with acne related to hormonal imbalances
  • Biologic therapies for conditions like psoriasis and eczema
  • Antihistamines for managing allergic skin reactions
