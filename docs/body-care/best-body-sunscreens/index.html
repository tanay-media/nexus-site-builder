Application Tips for Body Sunscreen

Proper application makes the difference between adequate protection and potential sun damage. Most people use only 25% of the recommended amount, leaving skin vulnerable to harmful UV rays.

01
Use enough product

Apply one ounce (two tablespoons) to cover your entire Body - that's enough to fill a shot glass.

02
Target forgotten areas

Pay special attention to your Chest, Hands, ears, and feet where sun damage often occurs first.

03
Reapply consistently

Reapply every two hours and immediately after swimming or sweating heavily.

Apply sunscreen 15-30 minutes before sun exposure to allow proper absorption. Your Hands need extra attention since frequent washing removes protection throughout the day. Don't forget often-missed spots like between your toes, behind your knees, and along your hairline - these areas can develop concerning dark spots or irregular patches that may cause worry about permanent damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What SPF level should I look for in body sunscreen?

You should look for broad spectrum SPF 30 or higher protection, which meets the American Academy of Dermatology's standards. This level provides effective protection against both UVA and UVB rays that cause immediate burns and long-term skin damage. SPF 30 or higher is considered non-negotiable for preventing both short-term and long-term skin damage.

How much sunscreen should I apply to my body?

You should apply approximately one ounce of sunscreen to cover the average adult's body surface area for effective protection. This amount ensures proper coverage of large skin areas including arms, legs, torso, and back. Using less than this recommended amount significantly reduces the effectiveness of sun protection.

What makes EltaMD UV Sport the best overall body sunscreen?

EltaMD UV Sport is zinc oxide-based and delivers broad-spectrum SPF 50 protection without feeling heavy or greasy. The water-resistant formula stays effective for 80 minutes of swimming or sweating, and it works immediately upon application by creating a physical barrier on your skin. It's also non-comedogenic, spreads easily across large areas, and is fragrance-free for sensitive skin types.

Is CeraVe Hydrating Sunscreen good for daily use on a budget?

Yes, CeraVe Hydrating Sunscreen is an excellent budget option at around $12 that makes daily sunscreen use financially sustainable. It provides SPF 30 protection and contains beneficial ingredients like ceramides and hyaluronic acid that help maintain your skin's protective barrier. The moisturizing formula is non-greasy, fragrance-free, and widely available at drugstores.

Why is zinc oxide better than chemical sunscreens for body protection?

Zinc oxide provides the most reliable protection against both UVA and UVB rays and works immediately upon application by creating a physical barrier on your skin's surface. Unlike chemical sunscreens that can irritate sensitive skin, mineral formulas like zinc oxide are gentler and less likely to cause reactions. This makes zinc oxide ideal for daily use on large body areas without the risk of skin irritation that might lead to inconsistent application.
